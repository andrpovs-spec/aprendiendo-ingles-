📄 Texto 2 — Artículo de revista

Learning English in Colombia: Why it matters more than ever

Colombia has made significant progress in English education over the past decade. According to recent reports, the number of Colombians who speak English at an intermediate level has doubled since 2015. However, experts say that there is still much work to be done, especially in rural areas and small cities.

Organizations like Club de la Gente in Fusagasugá are helping to close this gap by offering free English courses to community members. "We believe that language is the key to opportunity," says Carlos García, the organization's director. "When our students can communicate in English, they can access better jobs, international scholarships, and global networks."

The B1 level is particularly important because it is the minimum requirement for most Colombian universities and for many international visa applications. Experts recommend studying at least 300 hours of English to reach this level.

Producción escrita — Correo formal y redacción
El examen B1 real tiene dos tareas de escritura. Aquí practicas ambas. En el examen debes escribir entre 100 y 190 palabras por tarea.

Situación: Has received an email from a university in Spain asking about your English level and why you want to study there. Write an email responding to their questions. Include:
• Your current English level and how you learned it
• Why you want to study at their university
• Ask about scholarship opportunities

🗣️ Speaking — Parte 5 ⏱ Examen real: 12–17 min con examinador
Expresión oral — Guía de preparación
El Speaking es con un examinador real. No se puede simular en HTML, pero aquí tienes las 3 partes del examen y cómo prepararte.
Parte 1 — Interview (2–3 min)
El examinador te hace preguntas personales. Practica estas:
Q: "Tell me about yourself and your studies."
Habla de tu nombre, edad, estudios, trabajo y objetivos. 45–60 segundos.
Q: "Why are you learning English?"
Menciona universidad, trabajo, cooperación internacional. Sé específico.
Q: "What do you do in your free time?"
Usa Love/Like/Enjoy + ING. Describe 2–3 actividades con detalles.
Parte 2 — Long turn (3–4 min)
Te muestran una foto y debes describirla durante 1 minuto. Estructura sugerida:
1. Describe the scene: "In this photo, I can see..."
2. Describe people: "There is/are... They look... They seem to be..."
3. Speculate: "I think they might be... Perhaps they are..."
4. Connect to yourself: "This reminds me of... In my community..."
Parte 3 — Collaborative task (4–5 min)
Discutes con el otro candidato o con el examinador. Frases útiles:
Proponer:
"What do you think about...?"
Acordar:
"I agree with you because..."
Desacordar:
"I see your point, but..."
Concluir:
"So, shall we say that...?"

🎯 Consejos para el examen Cambridge B1 real
📖
Reading: Lee el texto completo primero. Luego lee las preguntas. Siempre vuelve al texto para confirmar — nunca uses solo tu memoria.
👂
Listening: Lee las preguntas ANTES del audio. Tienes tiempo. Subraya palabras clave. Escucharás el audio DOS veces en el examen real.
✍️
Writing: Planifica 5 minutos antes de escribir. Organiza tus ideas. Usa conectores (However, Furthermore, As a result). Revisa gramática al final.
🗣️
Speaking: Habla con confianza, no perfección. Los examinadores valoran la comunicación efectiva. Si no sabes una palabra, descríbela. No te quedes en silencio.
⏱️
Gestión del tiempo: Reading+Writing = 1h 20min. Listening = 35min. Speaking = 12–17min. No te quedes mucho en una sola pregunta.
🏛️
Inscripción en Colombia: British Council Bogotá (britishcouncil.org.co) o Centro Colombo Americano. El examen se hace con computador (Computer-based) o papel.
📅
Prepárate 3–6 meses: Con el curso de Club de la Gente (cartillas 0–19) como base, necesitas 3–6 meses adicionales de práctica intensiva para el examen oficial.
🎓
Ruta recomendada: B1 PET → B2 First (FCE) → C1 Advanced (CAE). Cada nivel abre más puertas académicas y profesionales a nivel mundial.
